## Introduction

This mixed berry tart recipe is perfect for summer. It's a sweet and refreshing dessert that's easy to make and will impress your guests. The combination of fresh berries and a buttery crust makes for a delicious and beautiful tart. Follow this easy recipe to create a perfect dessert for any occasion.

## Ingredients

Here are the ingredients you'll need to make this mixed berry tart for 4 portions:

- 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1 egg yolk
- 2 tbsp ice water
- 1 cup mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 tbsp cornstarch
- 1/4 cup cold water
- 1 tbsp lemon juice

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

2. In a large bowl, mix together flour, sugar, and salt. Add in the cubed butter and use your fingers to rub the mixture together until it resembles coarse crumbs.

3. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and ice water. Add the mixture to the flour mixture and stir until the dough comes together.

4. Press the dough into a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Use a fork to prick the bottom of the crust. Bake for 15 minutes or until lightly golden.

5. In a medium saucepan, combine the mixed berries, sugar, cornstarch, and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and the berries break down, about 5-7 minutes.

6. Remove the berry mixture from heat and stir in lemon juice. Pour the mixture into the pre-baked crust.

7. Bake the tart for an additional 10 minutes or until the filling is set. Allow to cool and chill in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving.

8. When ready to serve, remove the tart from the pan and place on a serving plate. Top with fresh berries and a dusting of powdered sugar.

## Variations

- Vegetarian: This recipe is already vegetarian, but you can make it vegan by using vegan butter and replacing the egg yolk with a vegan egg substitute.
- Gluten-free: To make this recipe gluten-free, replace the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.
- Mini tarts: Instead of making one large tart, you can make mini tarts by using a muffin tin. Press the dough into the muffin cups and fill with the berry filling. Bake for a shorter time, about 8-10 minutes.
- Different berries: You can use any combination of berries you like for this recipe. Try using strawberries or cherries for a different twist.
- Crust variations: For a nuttier flavor, you can replace 1/4 cup of the flour with almond flour. You can also add spices like cinnamon or nutmeg to the dough for a different flavor.
